## **4Ps of Marketing**

The **4Ps of Marketing**, also known as the **Marketing Mix**, are essential elements that a business uses to market its product effectively. They include **Product, Price, Place, and Promotion**. They help companies plan strategies to satisfy customer needs and achieve business goals.

---

### **1. Product**

A **product** refers to the goods or services offered to satisfy customer needs and wants. It includes features, design, quality, brand name, packaging, and after-sales service.

#### **Example:**

Apple‚Äôs **iPhone** has features like premium design, high-speed processor, camera quality, and strong brand value.

---

### **2. Price**

**Price** is the amount customers pay to purchase the product. Pricing decisions depend on cost, competition, demand, and customer perception. Price should match value and profitability.

#### **Example:**

* **Domino‚Äôs Pizza** uses competitive pricing and discount offers.
* **iPhone** uses premium pricing due to high brand value.

---

### **3. Place**

**Place** refers to where the product is made available to customers and how it is distributed. It includes distribution channels, locations, logistics, and delivery systems.

#### **Example:**

Amazon delivers products online to customers through warehouses and delivery partners. Domino‚Äôs delivers pizza through physical stores + online apps.

---

### **4. Promotion**

**Promotion** refers to the methods used to communicate and persuade customers to buy the product. It includes advertising, sales promotions, social media marketing, public relations, and personal selling.

#### **Example:**

Coca-Cola promotes through TV ads, festivals, sponsorships, social media campaigns, and discount schemes.
